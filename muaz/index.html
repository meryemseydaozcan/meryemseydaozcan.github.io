About

Leadership, culture, and the choice to lead.

I am a social and organizational psychologist studying leadership — in particular, why so many capable people are reluctant to step forward and lead. I completed my Ph.D. in Social and Organizational Psychology at Koç University in 2025, advised by Prof. Dr. Zeynep Aycan in the Koç University Leadership Lab.

My work centers on how people’s self-comparisons with existing leaders, the leadership culture around them, and gender and cultural cues shape their attitudes toward leadership and their decision to take it on. My dissertation introduced and tested how self-comparisons with existing leaders give rise to negative leadership attitudes, and my broader program develops a multilevel theory of leadership culture — the shared, often implicit understandings of what a leader looks like that quietly determine who self-selects into leadership.

Methodologically, I combine field studies, survey-based structural equation and multilevel models, and immersive experiments — including a virtual-reality project designed to reduce people’s worries about leadership.

Research

What I work on

Interconnected threads on how people come to see themselves — or not — as leaders.

01
🧭

Self-Comparisons & Leadership

How comparing oneself to existing leaders shapes perceptions of fit, negative leadership attitudes, and the choice to step forward — or hold back.

  • Self–leader comparison processes
  • Negative leadership attitudes
  • Reluctance to lead
02
🧩

Leadership Culture

The “bottleneck metaphor”: how shared, often implicit understandings of what leadership looks like develop, and how they constrain the diversity and effectiveness of leadership in groups.

  • Leadership culture cues
  • Self-selection into leadership
  • Multilevel theory building
03
🌍

Culture & Leadership Emergence

Cross-cultural perspectives on how and why leaders emerge — from paternalistic leadership to collectivist versus individualistic organizational contexts.

  • Cross-cultural psychology
  • Paternalistic leadership
  • Collectivism & individualism
04
⚖️

Gender & Leadership Stereotypes

How gendered cues embedded in leadership culture shape worries about leadership and perceptions of who counts as “leadership material.”

05
🥶

Immersive Methods (VR)

Using virtual reality and Wizard-of-Oz leadership simulations to study — and work to reduce — people’s worries about leadership.
